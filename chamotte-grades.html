Chamotte Grade Guide

From standard LF 42 to ultra-high alumina LF 70+ — every grade engineered for consistent chemistry, precise particle sizing and superior application performance.

🔶 Standard Chamotte
Shivmotte LF 42
The most versatile chamotte grade — widely used in refractory castables, ceramics bodies and foundry coatings.
  • Consistent Al₂O₃ ~42%, low iron content
  • Suitable for refractory castables and bricks
  • Used in ceramics bodies for improved green strength
  • Foundry coatings and core making
  • Available as 0–1 mm, 1–3 mm, 3–5 mm and 200 Mesh

🔶 High Alumina Chamotte
Shivmotte LF 46
Enhanced alumina chamotte for higher duty refractory applications requiring better thermal stability.
  • Al₂O₃ ~46%, improved refractoriness over LF 42
  • Used in high-duty castables and refractory bricks
  • Better thermal shock resistance than standard chamotte
  • Suitable for kiln furniture and precast shapes

🔷 High Alumina
Shivmotte LF 54
Mid-range high alumina chamotte ideal for demanding monolithic and dense refractory formulations.
  • Al₂O₃ ~54%, low silica and low iron
  • Suitable for dense refractory bricks and shapes
  • Excellent for cement plant and glass furnace applications
  • Good thermal shock and spalling resistance

💠 Super High Alumina
Shivmotte LF 60
Super high alumina chamotte for premium refractory products used in steel and cement industries.
  • Al₂O₃ ~60%, very low iron content
  • Excellent refractoriness — PCE SK 35–36
  • For critical steel plant and cement kiln applications
  • Suitable for high-strength premix castables
